Design the chain. Then the world happens to it.

Your firm sells a gadget for 75 per unit-batch. Production has three stages — Components → Assembly → Distribution — and each can be located in one of four regions with different costs, tariffs, and risk exposure. You may also dual-source any stage (+4 cost) to halve whatever shocks hit it.

After you lock the design, three real-world shocks hit in sequence: a port strike, a tariff war, and a pandemic. Cheapest is not safest — this is the efficiency-vs-resilience tradeoff every global value chain manages.

Score = your final profit vs. the best possible design (all 512 configurations evaluated against the same shocks).

How grading works: your grade for this arena is the average of your first two completed attempts.
