Your resources decide what you should sell to the world

Each round you are dealt a random endowment of capital (K) and labor (L). Two industries compete for them: Machinery is capital-intensive, Textiles is labor-intensive. You decide how much K and how much L goes to Machinery; the rest makes Textiles.

Everything sells at world prices, and your citizens spend their income optimally on both goods — so your only job is the production decision: squeeze the most value out of your endowment. Heckscher-Ohlin says a capital-abundant country earns most by tilting toward Machinery and exporting it. Test it.

Score = value you generate ÷ the maximum possible from your endowment, averaged over 3 rounds. After each round we check whether your exports matched the H-O prediction.
