Tariffs are a prisoner's dilemma. Play it against three rivals.

Each year, you and a rival country simultaneously choose Free Trade or Tariff. Payoffs per year: both free = 3/3 (gains from trade). You tariff while they don't = 5/0 (optimal-tariff grab). They tariff while you don't = 0/5. Both tariff = 1/1 (trade war — everyone loses).

You'll face three governments in sequence, each with a different temperament. Match lengths vary and are not announced. Cooperation is worth more than any clever betrayal over time — but only against rivals who remember.

Score = your total welfare relative to a diplomat who maintains free trade throughout (facing the same rivals and the same luck). This is why the WTO exists: it turns one-shot temptation into a repeated game with retaliation rules.

How grading works: your grade for this arena is the average of your first two completed attempts. One attempt counts fully on its own; a second averages in and can raise or lower it; later runs are practice. Unplayed arenas count as 0 toward the arena part of your course grade. Closing mid-game records nothing.
