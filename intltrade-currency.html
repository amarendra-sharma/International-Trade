Six quarters at the central bank's trade desk

You steer Meridia's exchange rate for six quarters. E is the real exchange rate — higher E = weaker currency = more competitive exports. The trade balance responds to E with a one-quarter lag, and a depreciation actually worsens the balance in the quarter you do it (imports get pricier immediately — the J-curve) before helping later.

Each quarter: cut rates (depreciate, but stoke inflation), raise rates (appreciate, cool inflation), spend scarce reserves to prop the currency, or hold. Welfare each quarter rewards a balanced trade account and punishes inflation above 4%. Two shocks are coming — an export slump in Q3 and an import-price spike in Q5.

Score = your total welfare against the mathematically best six-move sequence (computed by brute force over all 4,096 policies).

How grading works: your grade for this arena is the average of your first two completed attempts. One attempt counts fully on its own; a second averages in and can raise or lower it; later runs are practice. Unplayed arenas count as 0 toward the arena part of your course grade.
