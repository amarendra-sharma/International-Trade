Five firms. Five fates. Choose each one's global strategy.

You are a consultant. Five firms — each with a different productivity φ — ask the same question: serve only the home market, pay the fixed cost to export, build a plant abroad (FDI), or shut down?

Payoffs (per year, in millions): domestic profit = 1.2φ − 8. Exporting adds 0.9φ − 12 (shipping eats margin; 12 is the cost of foreign distribution). FDI instead adds 1.5φ − 24 (no shipping, but a whole plant). Shutting down = 0.

This is the Melitz model as a decision problem: only the productive export, only the very productive build abroad — not because managers differ, but because fixed costs sort firms. Score = value you create ÷ maximum possible.
