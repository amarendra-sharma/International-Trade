Price your variety as the market integrates

You run one firm among many in a monopolistically competitive industry — every firm sells its own variety of the same good. Your only lever is your price. Demand for your variety: a share of the market, minus lost customers if you price above the crowd, plus stolen customers if you undercut. You pay a fixed cost of 800 per year and 10 per unit.

Across 4 years, trade integration triples the market — but floods it with foreign rivals. Watch what integration does to markups, scale, and survival. Your score compares your cumulative profit with the best achievable pricing.

How grading works: your grade for this arena is the average of your first two completed attempts. One attempt counts fully on its own; a second averages in and can raise or lower it; later runs are practice. Unplayed arenas count as 0 toward the arena part of your course grade. Closing mid-game records nothing.
