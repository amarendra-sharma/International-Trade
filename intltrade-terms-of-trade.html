You are large enough to move world prices. That cuts both ways.

You govern Grandia, a large country exporting Ore and importing Consumer Goods. Because Grandia supplies a big share of the world ore market, everything you do moves the world price of ore — your terms of trade (price of exports ÷ price of imports).

Each of 6 years, pick one policy: expand the ore sector (more volume, but the extra world supply pushes ore prices down — grow too hard and you get immiserizing growth), build import-competing industry (import less → world price of imports falls in your favor), set a tariff (optimal-tariff logic: some improvement in TOT, but domestic distortion, and high tariffs risk retaliation), or invest in consumption.

Score = your cumulative national welfare relative to an expert policy sequence run on the same random events. Watch the TOT chart — it is the story of the game.
