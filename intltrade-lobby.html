Buy protection — when it's worth buying

You run an industry's lobbying office through 5 political seasons. Each season, protection is worth a known rent to you, and the government grants it if your contribution outweighs its political cost of annoying consumers and exporters (Grossman-Helpman's "protection for sale," playable).

The catch: you only see an estimate of the government's price — intelligence gives you a range of ±3. Contribute too little and you lose your money for nothing; too much and you overpay for a tariff you could have had cheaper. Sometimes the right answer is to keep your wallet shut.

Score = your net winnings vs. what a perfectly calibrated lobbyist would expect to win with the same intelligence.

How grading works: your grade for this arena is the average of your first two completed attempts. One attempt counts fully on its own; a second averages in and can raise or lower it; later runs are practice. Unplayed arenas count as 0 toward the arena part of your course grade. Closing mid-game records nothing.
